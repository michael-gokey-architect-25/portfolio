### Important Context for 2024–2026

Recent data from the *National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP)* and other literacy institutes indicates a few notable trends:

* **The "Literacy Gap":** While the average is ~240 wpm, nearly **54% of U.S. adults** read at or below a 6th-grade level, which often corresponds to a slower, more effortful reading pace (around 150–180 wpm).
* **Format Matters:** We tend to read about **20-30% slower on screens** than on physical paper due to "F-shaped" scanning patterns and eye fatigue.
* **Audiobooks:** Interestingly, most people listen to audiobooks at roughly **150–160 wpm** (the speed of natural speech), though many "power users" now speed them up to 1.5x to match their silent reading speed.

### Why 3 Minutes is the "Magic Number"

When you are behind a podium, you aren't just reading; you are communicating. To be effective, you need to account for:

* **The "Breathe and Pause" Factor:** You need time to breathe, pause for emphasis after a big point, and let the audience digest what you just said.
* **Audience Reaction:** If they laugh, clap, or even just murmur in agreement, you have to stop talking for a few seconds so you aren't "stepping" on their reaction.
* **Enunciation:** We speak slower to a crowd than we do to a friend. To ensure the person in the back row understands you, you'll naturally drop to about **130–150 words per minute.**

### The "Speechwriter’s Cheat Sheet"

If you are using that **500-word, single-spaced page** we discussed, here is how the timing typically breaks down:

| Pace | Words Per Minute | Time per 500-word Page |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Fast (Rushed)** | 160+ wpm | ~3 minutes |
| **Conversational (Ideal)** | **130–150 wpm** | **3.5 minutes** |
| **Formal/Slow** | 100–120 wpm | 4.5+ minutes |

> **Pro Tip:** Most professional speechwriters actually recommend **double-spacing** your script and using a **14pt or 16pt font**. It makes it much easier to keep your place when you look up to make eye contact. If you do that, one page will be about **250 words**, which equals exactly **2 minutes** of speaking time.
