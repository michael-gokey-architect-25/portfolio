### The "Seven Sensors" Breakdown (3-4 Minutes)

Instead of just listing them, categorize them by the **type of data** they generate for your AI models. This keeps the "System Architects" in the room engaged.

#### 1. The "Frequency & Velocity" Sensors

* **Points & Progress Bars:** These aren't just for the user. For your AI, these are **high-frequency telemetry**. They tell the system the *velocity* of a user’s engagement. Is the progress bar moving daily or once a week?
* **The Data Value:** Baseline engagement metrics for churn prediction.

#### 2. The "Milestone & Capability" Sensors

* **Badges & Levels:** These represent **state-changes**. A user moving from Level 1 to Level 2 isn't just "leveling up"; they are graduating into a new data bucket with different behavioral expectations.
* **The Data Value:** Feature engineering for user segmentation and "Mastery" modeling.

#### 3. The "Social & Competitive" Sensors

* **Leaderboards:** This is your **Social Signal**. Does a user work harder when they are #2 on a list? Or do they quit?
* **The Data Value:** Psychographic profiling. Your AI now knows if this specific user is motivated by *Status* or *Self-Improvement*.

#### 4. The "Intent & Narrative" Sensors

* **Challenges/Quests & Rewards:** These are the **Active Experiments**. You "nudge" a user with a specific Quest (e.g., "Walk to the South Hall") and a specific Reward (e.g., "50 bonus points").
* **The Data Value:** Reinforcement Learning (RL). Did the reward actually trigger the behavior? This is how you "close the loop" between a digital nudge and a physical action.
